Bio

Qian Lou is an assistant professor in Computer Science at the University of Central Florida. Before joining UCF, he worked as a Senior Research Scientist at Samsung AI Center in Mountain View, California. He develops open-source models, algorithms, and systems for evolving AI workloads, with a focus on efficiency, security, privacy, and trustworthiness, and he has published 100+ papers in top machine learning, security, and systems venues. His work has received a Best Paper Award at Samsung Research and Best Paper Finalist recognitions at PACT and ICCAD. His research has been supported by major federal programs, including NSF and DoD.

Research

My research builds models, algorithms, and systems to make evolving AI workloads, such as LLMs and agents, efficient and trustworthy. A list of my publications is available here.

Current Projects

  • Controllers and Generators for LLMs and Agents. We design efficient and trustworthy control layers such as routing and orchestration across hardware, experts, models, and agents. Representative examples include HW-Router (DAC 2026), SecureRoute (DAC 2026), and R2-Router (Rank #1 on RouterArena). On the generation side, we study new model architectures and compression methods, including DictFormer (ICLR 2022), Low-rank language model compression (ICLR 2022), and new MoE methods (TMLR 2026).
  • Safety Evaluation and Guardrails. We study how to disclose, evaluate, and mitigate security risks in LLMs and agents. Examples include prompt injection attacks on LLMs (NeurIPS 2023), certified security for language models (ACL 2024), and privacy guardrails through cryptography, including SHE (NeurIPS 2019), CipherPrune (ICLR 2025), and DictPFL (NeurIPS 2025).
  • Secure Computing via Cryptography. We develop practical cryptographic systems for AI, including verifiable FHE with DataSeal (S&P 2025), reliable FHE with ReliaFHE (ASPLOS 2026), universal support for linear and non-linear computation with Universal BGV (S&P 2026), and AI agents design/evaluation for FHE programming through FHE-Coder (ICLR 2026).

Older Projects

  • On-device Designs for AI and Cryptography. Earlier work focused on efficient on-device AI and cryptographic acceleration, including AutoQ (ICLR 2020) and Lite-MDETR (CVPR 2022).
